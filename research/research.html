Research

My current research at McMaster centers around using various algorithms and computer vision/image libraries to find sources and structures in two and three dimensional data sets.

The star forming clump NGC 1333 with the Perseus molecular cloud (Nebula) in far infrared.

The barrier mechanism of the electrostatic ion gauge barrier I designed and built at TRIUMF.

Orbits of the Jovian planets from N-body solar system simulation, Earth's orbit is unseen due to all the comets whizzing around.

Past research at Saint Mary's involved adapting the Discrete Correlation Coefficient (DCF) for use on UV and X-ray light curves from a particularly exciting active galaxy. For a computational methods class I built an N-body integrator to simulate the solar system evolution with and without the Jovian planets. I received a TRIUMF summer student scholarship in 2011 where I designed and built an electrostatic ion gauge barrier which mitigated interference on sensitive equipment.

I'm currently finishing my masters thesis on my work in Perseus, web updates are coming...
