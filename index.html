Somewhere, something incredible is waiting to be known.

     - Carl Sagan

Current data scientist at Shopify but a former astrophysics graduate student and winner of Father of the Year three years running at my address.

I earned my M.Sc. from McMaster University in 2014 analyzing giant molecular clouds (you might know them as nebula) for clues about how stars form and evolve. The above image is one such cloud in Perseus about 30 light years across; each bright point is a baby star still forming. In 2012 I earned my B.Sc. from Saint Mary's University using high energy observations to probe the active supermassive black holes that anchor galaxies.

I've done heaps of other cool stuff like working at the world's largest cyclotron, creating an accurate 3D simulation of the Solar System evolving, and giving public lectures at the W.J. McCallion Planetarium to name a few. I now spend my days now helping Shopify do amazing things with data.

I am endlessly fascinated by computers, both with what they can and can not do. I am also keenly interested in numerical programming, scientific computing and data science. I aim to adopt open science and am a cheerleader for open source software. I maintain a humble GitHub account with tools and programs I've developed and try to contribute to regularly.

Latest Interests

Using functional programming along with Apache Spark to embarrasingly paralellize all the things. Currently working on a seies of projects to teach former fellow STEM students how to use distributed computing for their own research.

An attempt at using React Native to build an astronomy app during the last Shopify hack days.
