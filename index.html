I am a graduate student at McMaster University working in Astrophysics. My current research involves analyzing giant molecular clouds for clues about how stars form and evolve. I work with and develop source, and structure finding algorithms on 2D & 3D observational data sets which borrow many techniques from computer vision.

Past areas of research have involved the sporadic, high intensity radiation from the super massive black holes at the centers of active galaxies. The last gasps of matter before the singularity are often in X-ray and UV energy regimes, often from within light hours of the black hole. I am also very interested in computer and numerical programming, and scientific computing. I aim to adopt open science practices by releasing my data, code, and results online whenever possible. I maintain a GitHub account with tools I've developed and use regularly.
