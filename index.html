I am a astrophysics graduate student at McMaster University. My current research involves analyzing giant molecular clouds (like the image above of a cloud about 30 light years across) for clues about how stars form and evolve. I work with and develop algorithms for finding sources (new stars) and structure (filaments that provide material to form a star) in 2D and 3D observational data sets. These methods borrow many techniques from computer vision and signal analysis.

My past areas of research have involved the sporadic, high intensity radiation from the super massive black holes at the centers of active galaxies. The last glimpses of light before matter falls into the singularity are often at X-ray and UV energies and can even come from within light-hours of the black hole event horizon!

I am also very interested in computer and numerical programming, and scientific computing. I aim to adopt open science practices by releasing my data, code, and results online whenever possible. I maintain a GitHub account with tools I've developed and use regularly.
